1. Why Fiber and Plant Diversity Matter

Feeding Your Microbial Ecosystem

Fiber and plant-based compounds serve as the primary fuel for diverse gut microbes. Different plants provide different types of fibers—resistant starches, soluble and insoluble fibers, polyphenols—each nourishing distinct bacterial species. This diversity underpins a resilient microbiome able to support digestion, mood, immunity, and more.

“While the amount of fiber is important, diversity is equally crucial… Each plant food… feeds a different microbial ‘team’.”
— Vogue health writer

Dr. Will Bulsiewicz compares a varied gut environment to the Amazon rainforest—rich, balanced, and vibrant.

The 30 Plants Weekly Goal

Evidence shows that consuming 30+ different plant foods per week is optimal for microbial richness:

  • The American Gut Project links ≥30 plant types with significantly more diverse microbiomes
  • Digestive health experts advocate diversity jars—containing nuts, seeds, spices—as easy tools to hit this goal

This approach supports SCFA production, immune modulation, improved metabolism, and better digestion.
